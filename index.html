History

Pokémon, also known as Pocket Monsters, is a Japanese media franchise founded by Ninteno, Gamefreak and Creatures.

The franchise was created by Satoshi Tajiri in 1995. The Pokémon series is centered around cathing "Pokémon", which humans (Pokémon Trainers) catch, train, play with and use for battling other Pokémon trainers.

The concept of Pokémon was conceptualized by Satoshi Tajajiri's childhood hobby of bug catching. This is also how Pokémon's main slogan "Gotta Catch 'Em All" was born.

Since its conceptualization, the Pokémon series has become the highest-grossing media franchise of all time.

Initially the Pokémon series started out as a video game series, but since then there have been various other forms of Pokémon media, such as: a card game, books, manga, comics, music, merchandise and a theme park.

Playing the Game

Pokémon Trainers are tasked with assignment of completing the Pokédex. The Pokédex is a digital journal of all the Pokémon the Pokémon Trainer has seen, caught or has battled on their journey. Completing the entire Pokédex is a monumental task, as there are over 800 Pokémon in existence.

Those who are interested in becoming the strongest Pokémon Trainer, ought to challenge the Gym Leaders. Gym Leaders are powerful Pokémon Trainers who are meant as a hurdle for the Pokémon Trainer to overcome, if they wish to become the very best. After a victory in a Pokémon battle, the Gym Leader awards the Trainer with a badge.

Each Pokémon region has always had 8 Gym badges that need to be collected. After collecting every single badge, the Pokémon Trainer must challenge the Elite Four, which are the 4 most powerful Pokémon Trainers of a region. After the Elite Four, the only other Trainer left to defeat is the Pokémon Challenger; the most powerful of all Pokémon Trainers.

What is a "Pokémon"?

A Pokémon is a creature, or Pocket Monster, which can be caught using a Pokéball. As the name suggest, a Pokéball is ball that stores a Pokémon within it. Pokémon can be released from these Pokéballs in order to battle other Pokémon.

Each Pokémon has a typing. A type represents a Pokémon elemental attribution. For example, Pikachu is an electric type, which means that Pikachu is capable of wielding the powers of thunder and lightning. In short, a Pokémon's type allows the Pokémon to use powerful types of attacks in battle.
